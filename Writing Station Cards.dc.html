Writing Station Cards

Six independent writing activities. One card out per week, matched to the week's Wonders text and skill. Every card produces writing that comes back — real evidence for the sort.
Label It, Then Say More
1. Draw this week's topic (or use the picture card).
2. Label three things — tap the sounds as you write.
3. Turn one label into a whole sentence.
4. Read it back with your finger under each word.
Comes back: your labeled picture + one sentence.
Finish the Starter
1. Read this week's sentence starter card.
2. Say your ending out loud first — whisper voice.
3. Write the whole sentence: capital, spaces, period.
4. Write a second sentence that says more.
Comes back: your two sentences.
Retell in Three
1. Think about this week's story.
2. Say the retell to a partner: first, then, last.
3. Write one sentence for each part.
4. Check: three capitals, three periods.
Comes back: your first–then–last page.
Question of the Week
1. Read the essential question card (from Wonders).
2. Say your answer with the starter: "I think … because …"
3. Write your answer — two sentences.
4. Underline the word that shows your reason (because).
Comes back: your answer with because underlined.
Fix This Sentence
1. Read the broken sentence card (no capital, no period…).
2. Be the teacher — find what's missing.
3. Rewrite it the right way.
4. Write your own sentence with everything it needs.
Comes back: the fixed sentence + your own.
Copy the Model, Make It Mine
1. Read the model sentence from this week's modeled writing.
2. Copy it in your best handwriting.
3. Change one part to make it yours (who, what, where).
4. Read both sentences to a partner.
Comes back: the model + your version.
Station setup: every card says it before writing it — oral rehearsal is the EL access point • post the complete-sentence anchor chart and this week's starter card at the station • the returned writing sorts straight into mastered / approaching / needs access point for Session 5.

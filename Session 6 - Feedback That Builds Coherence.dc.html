PART 1

The Strength-Based Frame

THE FRAME • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

"Because You …, Your Students Were Able To …"

Praise without a handle
"Great lesson! The kids were so engaged. I loved your energy."
Kind — but nothing to repeat tomorrow. No move named, no outcome tied to it.
A frame that builds practice
"Because you had partners rehearse the sentence before the share-out, 16 of 18 students gave a complete sentence — including two of your ELs."
A named move, a counted outcome — worth repeating, worth spreading.
The frame ties a teacher move to a student outcome — the same cause-and-effect our whole system runs on.
FROM THE FS IPG ALIGNMENT GUIDES • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

Then One Reflective Question

Who participated in that activity — and who found a way to be present without practicing?
Which access point did the most work in this lesson? How do you know?
Where did students struggle productively — and where did the struggle stop being productive?
If you taught this lesson again tomorrow, which scaffold would you keep, fade, or add?

Draft and Rehearse

WORKING TIME • 30 MINUTES • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 3

From Observation Notes to a Delivered Frame

1
Find the move in your notes — a routine, annotation, or scaffold you saw working.
2
Tie it to the outcome — what students were able to do, counted where you can.
3
Choose one reflective question from the menu to follow the strength.
4
Rehearse both roles — deliver it, receive it, refine the wording together.

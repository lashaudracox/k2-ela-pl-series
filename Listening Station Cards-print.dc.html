Listening Station Cards

Six listen-and-respond activities for the Wonders audio texts. Listening is participation too — every card ends with a response that comes back.
Listen & Draw
1. Listen to the whole story — headphones on, voice off.
2. Draw your favorite part.
3. Label two things in your picture.
4. Write one sentence: "My favorite part was … because …"
Comes back: your labeled picture + sentence.
Stop & Predict
1. Listen to the first half — pause at the sticker mark.
2. Say your prediction to a partner: "I think … will …"
3. Draw or write your prediction.
4. Listen to the end. Circle: Was I right? YES / NO / CLOSE
Comes back: your prediction with the circle.
Character Feelings
1. Listen for how the character feels.
2. Draw the character's face at the beginning and at the end.
3. Write: "___ felt ___ when ___."
4. Tell a partner what changed the feeling.
Comes back: your two faces + sentence.
Word Catcher
1. Look at this week's two power words before you listen.
2. Listen — tap the table each time you hear one.
3. Write both words.
4. Use one in your own sentence — say it, then write it.
Comes back: both words + your sentence.
First, Then, Last
1. Listen to the whole story.
2. Fold your paper into three boxes.
3. Draw first, then, last — one box each.
4. Retell the story to a partner using your boxes.
Comes back: your three-box retell.
Answer the Question
1. Read the question card before you listen.
2. Listen for the answer — thumbs up when you hear it.
3. Say your answer with the starter: "I learned that …"
4. Write your answer in one sentence.
Comes back: your answer sentence.
Station setup: use the week's Wonders audio (main selection or read-aloud) • teach the headphone-and-pause routine whole-group first • the question, power words, and pause point come from your annotated lesson • listening without a response is invisible — the "comes back" work is how this station lands on the rotation chart.

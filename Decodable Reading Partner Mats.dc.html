Decodable Reading Partner Mats

Print one mat per partnership (landscape), laminate, and keep at the decodable reading station. Page 1 sits between the partners; page 2 is the teacher card.

Partner Reading Mat
Book between us • both eyes on the words • reader's finger under the word
Reader (A)
1. Read a page out loud.
2. Stuck? Tap the sounds and blend — slow, then fast.
3. Still stuck? Ask your coach for a clue, not the word.
4. Reread the sentence smoothly before turning the page.
Coach (B)
1. Follow along — eyes on every word.
2. Wait. Give your reader time to try.
3. Give clues: "Tap it." • "Look at every letter." • "What's the tricky part?"
4. Say "Nice fix!" when your reader works it out.
Switch jobs at the end of the book — or when the timer sounds. Both partners read every day.
Teacher Card — Making Partner Reading Count
Why coach clues, not answers: telling the word ends the practice; a clue keeps the reader decoding (Core Action 3C — productive struggle). Teach the three clues explicitly and role-play both jobs before the first rotation.
Pairing for access: pair by cooperation, not just level — a reader two steps ahead makes a patient coach. ELs pair with strong language models. Rotate pairs when the sort re-forms groups.
The count: both partners' eyes on the words IS the participation look-for. Listen in on 2–3 pairs per rotation; tally who reads, who coaches with clues, who waits it out.
What comes back: a simple tally strip — pages read, one "nice fix" word the coach heard. That strip is the station's accountability check on the rotation chart.

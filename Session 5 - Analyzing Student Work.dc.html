PART 1

The Sorting Protocol

SORTING PROTOCOL • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

Three Piles, and What Each One Asks of Us

Mastered
The work meets the task. Ask: what's the stretch — fluency, more volume, a harder application?
Approaching
Nearly there. Ask: what one error pattern, if fixed, closes the gap? Often a quick whole-group reinforce.
Needs access point
The lesson didn't reach them yet. Ask: which access point was missing — and how does the reteach supply it?
Not "low group" — "needs access point." The label keeps the next move on our side of the table.
FROM VAGUE TO ACTIONABLE

Name the Need Precisely

Too vague to act on
  • "Struggles with reading"
  • "Can't write yet"
  • "Wasn't paying attention"
  • "Low"
Named — and teachable
  • Stumbles on tricky words: said, the, was
  • Confuses digraphs ch / sh in decoding and spelling
  • Writes labels, not complete sentences
  • Decodes accurately but loses the sentence by the end
PART 2

Sort, Name, Reteach

WORKING TIME • 35 MINUTES • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 3

From a Class Set to a Reteach Plan

1
Sort every paper — mastered / approaching / needs access point. Record the counts.
2
Name the specific need on each "needs access point" paper — with your ESL/SPED partner's read.
3
Group by need, not by level — the ch/sh group, the complete-sentence group.
4
Plan one small-group reteach: the access point it supplies, and the lesson slot it lives in this week.

Quarter 2: From Access to Independence

Biweekly K–2 ELA planning • ~60 minutes • Presenter: LaShaudra Cox, Ross Elementary • Final focus set by the Session 7 team commitments (Thu, Oct 22).

The Through-Line Continues

Quarter 1 built the system: routines that create access, lessons annotated with access points, participation made visible, scaffolds matched to evidence, work sorted, feedback delivered, data reviewed. Quarter 2 deepens it — students carry more of the work themselves, scaffolds fade on evidence, and writing takes a larger share of the lens. Same system: we plan it, we teach it, we see it, we measure it.

What carries over

Every session still opens with last session's evidence and closes with a commitment. ESL & SPED partners join S9 and S11. Walkthrough look-fors extend — same checklist, plus fade evidence and writing participation.

How we'll know it's working

Participation holds at Q1's gains with fewer scaffolds in place; writing-task mastery grows across the sorted sets; winter screening confirms movement; Tier II referrals for missing Tier I access keep falling.

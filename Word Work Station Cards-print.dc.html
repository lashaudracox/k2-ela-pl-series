Word Work Station Cards

Six independent activities for the word work station. Print, cut, laminate — put out ONE card at a time with this week's word list. Every card ends with something that comes back.
Build It, Tap It, Write It
1. Build a word from the list with letter tiles.
2. Tap each sound with your finger.
3. Mix the tiles up — write the word from memory.
4. Check against the list. Fix, then next word.
Comes back: your written word list, checked.
Sound Sort
1. Read each word card out loud — quiet voice.
2. Sort into the two columns on the mat (this week's sounds).
3. Read each column top to bottom to check.
4. Write two words from each column.
Comes back: your four written words.
Change One Sound
1. Start with the first word (example: cat).
2. Change one letter to make a new word (hat, cap…).
3. Write your word chain — as long as you can.
4. Read your whole chain to a station partner.
Comes back: your word chain.
Tricky Word Detective
1. Pick a tricky word card.
2. Write it. Circle the regular sounds in blue.
3. Heart the tricky part in red.
4. Use it in a sentence you say to your partner.
Comes back: three hearted words.
Rainbow Write
1. Write each list word in pencil, saying the sounds.
2. Trace it in two more colors — say the sounds each time.
3. Cover it. Write it once more from memory.
4. Check and fix.
Comes back: your rainbow page with the memory try.
Sentence Builder
1. Choose two words from the list.
2. Say a sentence with each — partner first.
3. Write your best sentence: capital, spaces, period.
4. Read it back with your finger under each word.
Comes back: your two sentences.
Station setup: teach each card whole-group before it goes in the station — model, practice, reinforce • one card per week, matched to the week's Wonders sound-spelling and word lists • the "comes back" work lands in the station tray and counts on the rotation chart's accountability column.

Anchor Charts That Create Access

Sketches to recreate on chart paper — build each one WITH students during the lesson, then point to it in the moment.
Sound Spotlight: /ee/ vs /ae/
ee
feet  •  see  •  tree
smile wide: "eeee"
a_e
cake  •  gate  •  name
open jaw: "aaay"

Use with: the Session 2 worked example — students compare the two columns in a Turn & Talk. Add student-suggested words as you go.

Digraph Duo: ch / sh
ch
chip  •  lunch  •  chat
train sound: "ch-ch-ch"
sh
ship  •  fish  •  she
quiet sound: finger to lips

Use with: the Session 5 reteach group whose sort showed ch/sh confusion. Two letters, one sound — underline the team.

A Complete Sentence Has…
1. A who or what  (The dog)
2. A did or does  (runs fast)
3. A capital to start, a period to stop
The dog runs fast.

Use with: the "writes labels, not sentences" need. Students check their own sentence against the three points before sharing.

Turn & Talk Looks Like / Sounds Like
Looks like
Knee to knee
Eyes on partner
One voice at a time
Sounds like
"I think … because …"
"Can you say more?"
"I agree / disagree because …"

Use with: the Session 1 discourse routines — model it, practice it, point to it every Turn & Talk in weeks 1–3.

Tricky Word Wall: said • was • the
said
s-ai-d
the tricky part is ai
was
w-a-s
the tricky part is a
the
th-e
the tricky part is e

Use with: "stumbles on tricky words" needs. Heart the tricky part; students tap the regular sounds and learn the tricky part by sight.

When I'm Stuck on a Word…
1. Look at every letter, left to right
2. Say each sound — tap it out
3. Blend it: slow, then fast
4. Ask: does it make sense?

Use with: productive struggle (Session 3) — the teacher's prompt points here, to the sound-spelling, never to the answer.

Three rules for any anchor chart: build it with students, not before them • hang it where the lesson happens and point to it in the moment • take it down when the sort says the need is met — an anchor chart is a scaffold, and scaffolds are temporary.

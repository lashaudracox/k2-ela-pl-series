PART 1

The Core Action 3 Indicators

FS IPG • CORE ACTION 3 • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

Three Indicators, One Word That Matters: ALL

A
ALL students participate in the work of the lesson — listening, speaking, reading, and writing — not just volunteers.
B
ALL students have access to the text and task — decodables in hand, materials ready, directions understood.
C
ALL students do the thinking — productive struggle is supported, not shortcut by the teacher doing the work.
The walkthrough question all quarter: who is doing the work — and who is watching others do it?
PART 2

Making Participation Visible

YOUR SESSION 1 ROUTINES, NOW AS EVIDENCE

Structures That Let You See Every Student

Hand signals
Every student shows the sound, the count, or agreement — a glance tells you who is with you.
Whiteboards
All students write, all boards go up. Blank boards are data too — they tell you who needs an access point.
Choral response
On the signal, every voice — and your ear picks out who is rehearsing and who is mouthing along.
INDICATORS B & C IN PRACTICE

Decodable Text Access and Productive Struggle

Access slips away when…
  • The teacher reads the decodable aloud first — students echo, never decode
  • One student answers while eighteen wait
  • A struggling reader gets the answer, not a strategy
  • The task is swapped for an easier one instead of scaffolded
Struggle stays productive when…
  • Every student has the text in hand and a job to do with it
  • Wait time + partner rehearsal come before the share-out
  • Prompts point to the sound-spelling, not the answer
  • The grade-level task stays; the support scaffolds up to it

Note who wasn't in the count — by name. They are Session 4's scaffolding work.

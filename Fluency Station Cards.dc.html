Fluency Station Cards

Six re-reading activities for decodables and familiar texts. Fluency grows on re-reads of text students can already decode — accuracy first, then speed, then expression.
Three Reads
1. Read the page once — get every word right.
2. Read it again — smooth, like talking.
3. Read it a third time — with feeling.
4. Color one star on your strip for each read.
Comes back: your three-star strip with the page number.
Echo Read
1. Partner A reads a sentence like a storyteller.
2. Partner B echoes it — same words, same voice.
3. Switch every page.
4. Pick your best sentence and echo it for the tray recording sheet.
Comes back: your best-sentence line, both names.
Punctuation Voice
1. Hunt the page: circle . ? ! on your copy.
2. Practice: period = stop • question = voice up • ! = strong voice.
3. Read the page making every mark work.
4. Partner checks: did the voice match the mark?
Comes back: your circled page with the partner check.
Beat the Clock (Gently)
1. Read the word list or page. Partner starts the sand timer.
2. Mark where you stopped when the sand ran out.
3. Read it again — try to pass your mark, no skipped words.
4. Write both marks: read 1 ____ • read 2 ____.
Comes back: your two marks — beating yourself, not others.
Phrase Builder
1. Read the phrase cards (2–3 words each) in a whisper.
2. Read each one again like talking — no robot voice.
3. Find two of the phrases in your decodable.
4. Read those whole sentences smoothly to a partner.
Comes back: the two sentences, page numbers written.
Perform It
1. Pick your best-known page from this week.
2. Practice it three times — smooth and strong.
3. Perform it for your station group.
4. Group gives one star comment: "Your voice …"
Comes back: your page number + the star comment.
Station setup: only familiar text goes in this station — decodables already read at the teacher table • accuracy before speed: a fast wrong read practices errors • the timer card races the student's own mark, never a classmate • re-read counts and star strips are fluency evidence for the sort and the data review.

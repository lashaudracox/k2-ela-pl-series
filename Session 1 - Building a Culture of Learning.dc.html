OUR NORTH STAR • INSTRUCTIONAL COHERENCE

One System, Not Many Initiatives

We PLAN it
Access points are annotated into lessons during biweekly ELA planning — with ESL & SPED partners at the table.
We TEACH it
The scaffolds we planned — visuals, discourse, modeling — show up in daily instruction in every K–2 room.
We SEE it
Walkthroughs look for the same thing planning planned: who is listening, speaking, reading, writing.
We MEASURE it
Student work and participation data come back to planning — and shape the next access points.
PART 2

The FS IPG Culture of Learning

FS IPG • CULTURE OF LEARNING • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

Five Descriptors of a Strong Culture of Learning

1
Students complete instructional tasks, volunteer responses, and ask appropriate questions.
2
Students follow behavioral expectations and directions.
3
Students execute transitions, routines, and procedures in an orderly and efficient manner.
4
Students are engaged in the work of the lesson from start to finish — with a sense of urgency about time.
5
Students and their teacher demonstrate a joy for learning through positive relationships responsive to student interests and experiences.
TURN & TALK • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 2

Barriers and Enablers in Our Routines

When culture becomes a barrier…
  • Only volunteers ever answer
  • Directions given once, orally, in English only
  • Materials passed out mid-activity; time lost
  • "Good" behavior = silent and still
  • No routine for how partners talk
When culture creates access…
  • Choral response & hand signals — every voice
  • Directions shown with visuals & modeled
  • Materials staged; transitions rehearsed
  • Engagement — not silence — is the expectation
  • Turn & Talk partners assigned and practiced
PART 3

Routines That Create Access

PLAN IT LIKE PHONICS: MODEL → PRACTICE → REINFORCE • PARTICIPANT GUIDE P. 3

Participation Routines to Teach in Weeks 1–3

Every-Voice Routines
  • Choral response signal
  • Hand signals for sounds & agreement
  • Whiteboards: all students write
  • Sound-tapping / finger segmenting
Discourse Routines
  • Turn & Talk with assigned partners
  • Sentence starters posted
  • "Say it with your partner first"
  • Random pair share-outs
Access Routines
  • Anchor charts introduced & referenced
  • Visual directions — icons + words
  • Materials staged before lessons
  • Rehearsed small-group transitions

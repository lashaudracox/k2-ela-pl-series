Session 1 — Participant Guide
K–2 ELA PROFESSIONAL LEARNING • SESSION 1 • PARTICIPANT GUIDE

Building a Culture of Learning

Access for ALL students from Day One — grounded in the TN Foundational Skills IPG Culture of Learning.
Presenter: LaShaudra Cox
School: Ross Elementary
When: Inservice Week
Time: 60 minutes

Today's Learning Objectives

  • Define instructional coherence and explain why intentional preparation creates access to grade-level learning.
  • Explore the FS IPG Culture of Learning descriptors and distinguish compliance from true participation.
  • Connect our first-three-weeks routines and procedures to access for every learner, including ELs and students with IEPs.
  • Commit to one participation routine to explicitly teach during weeks 1–3.

The Five Culture of Learning Descriptors — Notes

As we review each descriptor, jot what it would look like and sound like in your classroom.

Descriptor (students…) Looks like / sounds like in my room
Complete tasks, volunteer responses, ask appropriate questions
Follow behavioral expectations and directions
Execute transitions, routines, and procedures efficiently
Are engaged start to finish, with urgency about time
Show joy for learning through positive relationships responsive to student interests and experiences

PART 2 • TURN & TALK

Barriers and Enablers in Our Routines

Think about a routine you already teach in weeks 1–3. Does it open participation for your ELs and students with IEPs — or quietly close it? Record one of each with your partner.

A routine that could become a BARRIER — and why A routine that CREATES ACCESS — and why

Remember: "quiet" can meet the behavior descriptor while missing participation and engagement. A routine is a scaffold when it is taught, visible, and practiced — just like phonics.

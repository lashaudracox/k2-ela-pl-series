K–2 ELA PROFESSIONAL LEARNING • SESSIONS 1 & 3 • HAND SIGNAL CARDS

Hand Signal Cards

Print, cut, and post by the rug. Every signal is an answer every student gives at once — that's what makes participation visible and countable.

Thinking Signals

I agree
Thumb up, held at your chest
I disagree
Thumb down, held at your chest
Me too / same idea
Thumb and pinky out, gentle shake
Still thinking
Fist on chest — "give me a moment"

Sound Signals

How many sounds?
Show that many fingers — c-a-t = 3
Tap the sounds
Finger taps thumb for each sound, left to right
I hear it!
Hand taps ear when you hear the target sound
Blend it
Hands apart for sounds → slide together for the word

Celebration & Help Signals

Silent cheer
Both hands up, sparkle fingers — no sound
I need help
Hand up with a question-mark finger hook
Teaching the signals: introduce two at a time, not all ten • model, practice as a game, reinforce — like phonics • signals at the chest keep them private enough that slower thinkers still answer • a sea of signals is your ten-second participation count: who signals, who copies a neighbor, who waits.

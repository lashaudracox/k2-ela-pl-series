ONE COHERENT SYSTEM

The Through-Line

We PLAN it
Access points annotated into lessons in biweekly ELA planning.
We TEACH it
The planned scaffolds show up in daily K–2 instruction.
We SEE it
Walkthroughs look for what planning planned — participation.
We MEASURE it
Student work and counts come back to planning and shape the next cycle.
What we plan is what we teach, what we look for, what we name in feedback, and what we measure — one system, not many initiatives.

FOUR CONCRETE ASKS

What We Ask of Leadership

1
Protect the block — keep the biweekly K–2 ELA planning time intact through October.
2
Schedule the partners — ESL and SPED staff free to join Sessions 2, 4, and 5.
3
Align walkthroughs — look for participation (listening, speaking, reading, writing) and count it.
4
Name it in feedback — use the strength-based frame: "Because you …, your students were able to …"
REVIEWED TOGETHER IN SESSION 7 • OCT 22

How We Will Measure Success

Participation
Walkthrough counts of students listening, speaking, reading, writing trend toward all — from baselines like 11/18 and 14/18.
Mastery
The share of students mastering lesson writing and decoding tasks grows across the quarter — baseline 10/18.
Systems
Fewer Tier II referrals driven by missing Tier I access points; scaffolds visible in annotated lessons.

One system: plan it, teach it, see it, measure it.

By October 22, our own data tells us whether access grew.

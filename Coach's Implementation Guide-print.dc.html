Running the Series: A Coach's Guide

How the seven sessions, the between-session coaching, and the classroom tools work as one system — and what to do when they don't.
Series lead: LaShaudra Cox
School: Ross Elementary
Cadence: Biweekly, 60 min, inside K–2 ELA planning

The Design in One Paragraph

Every session teaches one practice, ends with a classroom commitment, and opens the next session with the evidence that commitment produced. The chain is deliberate: routines (S1) → annotated access points (S2) → visible, counted participation (S3) → scaffolds matched to evidence (S4) → sorted student work and reteach (S5) → peer feedback that names what worked (S6) → a data review that decides Quarter 2 (S7). Your job as coach is to protect the chain — the session content matters less than the evidence moving through it.

Non-Negotiables

  • Open with their evidence, every time. The first ten minutes belong to the exit tickets from last session. Skip this once and commitments become optional.
  • Working time is sacred. Each session's largest block is teachers working in their own Wonders maps and student work — never let the presentation eat it.
  • Counts, not vibes. Model the language relentlessly: 14/18, three moved piles, two still outside the count. Celebrate in the same units.
  • ESL & SPED partners are co-planners, not guests. Confirm coverage for S2/S4/S5 a week ahead; seat them inside grade teams.
  • Same lens everywhere. The walkthrough checklist, the feedback frames, and the session content use identical language. If leadership walkthroughs drift, realign them before the next session.

Between Sessions — Where Implementation Lives

Window Coach moves
After S1 → S2
weeks 1–3		Visit every K–2 room once; look only for the committed routine being taught (model–practice–reinforce). Leave one strength-based note per teacher. Deliver classroom kits (posters, signal cards, partner cards).
After S2 → S3Check that annotated lessons get taught — offer to co-teach or model one. Collect a photo of one strong annotation per grade (with permission) for the S3 opener.
After S3 → S4Do your own participation counts in walkthroughs using the look-for checklist; the numbers seed the S4 baseline comparison. Nudge teachers who haven't counted yet — ten seconds, one activity.
After S4 → S5Watch one scaffolded lesson per grade. Remind teams to keep the FULL class set from the scaffolded lesson — the S5 sort needs every paper, not the best ones.
After S5 → S6Broker the peer observations: build the coverage schedule, pair observers across grades where possible, hand each pair the observation form. Video is a legitimate fallback.
After S6 → S7Pull the quarter data (walkthrough counts, screening, RTI² referrals) so teams don't spend S7 hunting numbers. Pre-fill the August baselines on the team data summaries. Fill in certificates.

Troubleshooting

If you see… Try…
Teachers arrive without evidence (no count, no work set)Don't scold — pair them with a teammate who brought theirs for the debrief, then make a specific plan and date with them before they leave. Track it on the sign-in sheet's evidence column.
Annotations that say "differentiate" or "support"Push to the frame: which move (visual / discourse / model / prior knowledge), which students, what will you make or say? The worked /ee/ example is the bar.
Counts stuck at the baseline for a teamObserve the counted activity yourself — usually the structure isn't visible (no boards up, volunteers answering). Reteach the every-voice routine with the teacher, then recount.
The sort produces one big "low" pileThe task, not the students: check the lesson was taught at grade level with the planned access points. Re-sort by named need — the pile always splits.
Peer feedback that's all praise, no frameRehearse it live in S6 and accept only framed feedback on the exit ticket: a named move + a counted outcome. "Great lesson!" gets a warm redo.
Leadership walkthroughs looking for complianceBring the walkthrough look-for checklist to admin team; re-anchor on the kickoff deck's four asks. One misaligned walkthrough undoes a session of trust.

Reporting Up

  • After S1: send leadership the kickoff deck and the four asks (protect the block, schedule partners, align walkthroughs, use the frame).
  • Monthly: one paragraph — participation counts vs. baseline, one bright spot in frame language, one support needed.
  • After S7: the team data summaries ARE the quarter report. Attach the Q2 outline with the focus the teams chose.
The coach's through-line: you are not running seven meetings — you are keeping one promise: that what teachers plan together is what they teach, what everyone looks for, what feedback names, and what the data measures. Every coaching decision should make that promise easier to keep.
